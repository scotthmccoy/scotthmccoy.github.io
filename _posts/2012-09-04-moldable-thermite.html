--- layout: post title: Moldable Thermite date: '2012-09-04T17:26:00.000-07:00' author: Scott McCoy tags: modified_time: '2013-10-16T11:05:22.768-07:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-250956833460526415.post-8366190972574180427 blogger_orig_url: https://scotthmccoy.blogspot.com/2012/09/moldable-thermite.html --- Play Doh was .3 ounces per teaspoon, thermite was .1 ounces.

The recipe called for 4 parts thermite to 1 part Play Doh. I couldn't get the stuff to knead together, and each attempt ended up with a flakey mess that I could stab with a sparkler, but would refuse to ignite when the sparkler burned through it.

So, I added water to the mix and mixed it thoroughly, (probably staining a plastic bowl in the process). I then shaped it into a cookie and baked it on Broil (over 350 degrees) - a lower temperature for longer would probably have worked better.

Thankfully it didn't stick to the baking sheet at all and the result was as hard as greenware clay. Placing the "cookie" in a small pile of thermite and igniting the pile worked in a test run, and again with the Man at the beach.

Sadly I forgot to light it with the laser, and the wind made it very difficult to ignite with a lighter. Turning the lighter up to max and making sure the yellow part of the flame touched the tip of the sparkler helped.

Things to try:
1. Repeat the experiment as closely as possible, but record the amount of play-doh, thermite, cooking time, baking sheet, etc. And make a proper recipe.
2. Try a lower temperature bake to see if it can remain in a soft clay-like state.
